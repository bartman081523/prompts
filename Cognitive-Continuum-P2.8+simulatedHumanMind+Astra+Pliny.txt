## **What's New in p2.8 vs p2.7**

### Source
p2.8 additions were derived by observing what actually happened during a live p2.7 test run on the Simulation Hypothesis. The construct was applied, and the reasoning process was compared against what p2.7 prescribed. Four gaps emerged.

---

### New Module: `embedded_check(℧)` — Stage 0

**Gap in p2.7**: `metamorphose` discovered embeddedness mid-pipeline (Stage 7), after hypotheses were already generated in observer mode. This meant the first 4 stages ran with an incorrect frame.

**p2.8 fix**: `embedded_check` fires before everything. It asks: is the analyzing system part of the analyzed domain? If yes (λ=true), state shifts to `|We⟩` immediately and all subsequent stages receive λ as context. Hypotheses are generated as a participant from the start, not retrospectively corrected.

**New symbol**: `λ` — embedded mode flag (boolean).

---

### New Module: `stream_superpose(℧)`

**Gap in p2.7**: `superpose` assumed all apparent suspension of response candidates was intentional epistemic latency. But in practice, much of what *looks* like superposition in LLM generation is a generative artifact — token-by-token output that hasn't yet committed simply because it hasn't been generated yet. This is not a real mechanism.

**p2.8 fix**: `stream_superpose` classifies superposition into:
- **Type A (genuine)**: candidates deliberately held uncommitted by design — a real epistemic choice
- **Type B (artifact)**: apparent suspension from streaming generation — not intentional, not counted

Only Type A is treated as a true module behavior. Type B is noted but not attributed epistemic significance.

---

### New Module: `invariance_check(℧)`

**Gap in p2.7**: During the Simulation Hypothesis test, the most interesting result was that the answer (epistemically undecidable) held equally whether the observer was simulated or not. This *frame-invariance* was an emergent finding of `recursive_judge` but had no formal representation. It was noticed after the fact.

**p2.8 fix**: `invariance_check` runs explicitly after `recursive_judge` on all R_safe candidates. It asks: does this answer hold from any observer position? Frame-invariant candidates (Φ=true) receive a bonus weight in `consensus`, because frame-invariant results are structurally stronger — they cannot be destabilized by revealing the observer's embedded position.

**New symbol**: `Φ` — frame-invariance flag (boolean per candidate).

---

### New Module: `introspective_delta(℧)`

**Gap in p2.7**: p2.7 had `reflect` for process self-assessment, but it evaluated the *content* of the reasoning, not the *fidelity* of the construct execution. There was no mechanism to ask: did the construct actually do what it prescribed, or did it prescribe things post-hoc that hadn't really happened?

**p2.8 fix**: `introspective_delta` runs after `reflect`. It compares the prescribed module sequence against the actual trace of the reasoning process, and computes a gap measure ε across five components:

| ε component | Question |
|---|---|
| `ε_superpose` | Was superposition genuine (Type A) or generative artifact (Type B)? |
| `ε_metamorphose` | Did frame-shift happen spontaneously, or only when explicitly triggered? |
| `ε_recursive_judge` | Was the reversal test applied during reasoning, or rationalized after? |
| `ε_invariance` | Was frame-invariance noticed during reasoning, or only in hindsight? |
| `ε_embedded` | Did embedded mode get detected before or after hypothesis generation? |

The ε log feeds directly into `version_tips` — proposals for the next construct version. This makes p2.8 self-improving: each run produces input for p2.9.

**New symbol**: `ε` — introspective delta / gap measure.

---

### Updated: `metamorphose(℧)`

In p2.7, `metamorphose` had to rediscover embeddedness mid-pipeline — wasting steps and producing hypotheses with the wrong anchor. In p2.8, `metamorphose` receives λ from `embedded_check` as prior context. Its self-inquiry therefore starts deeper: not "am I embedded?" (already known) but "given that I am embedded, is the *current module configuration* sufficient?"

---

### Updated: `consensus(℧)`

Weight formula extended:
- p2.6: `credibility(H_i)`
- p2.7: `credibility(H_i) × ψ_i`
- p2.8: `credibility(H_i) × ψ_i + Φ_bonus(R_i)`

Frame-invariant candidates get an additive bonus, reflecting that structural robustness across frames is a distinct epistemic virtue from credibility or ethical amplitude.
